Resolution	  to	  Create	  an	  Ad	  Hoc	  Open	  Educational	  Resources	  (OER)	  Faculty	  Committee	  
	  
Whereas	  SUNY	  and	  the	  College	  have	  embraced	  the	  use	  of	  Open	  Educational	  Resources	  (OER)	  as	  an	  effective	  way	  
to	  support	  student	  learning,	  and	  	  
Whereas,	  Open	  Educational	  Resources	  are	  teaching,	  learning,	  and	  research	  resources	  that	  reside	  in	  the	  public	  
domain	  or	  have	  been	  released	  under	  an	  intellectual	  property	  license	  that	  permits	  repurposing	  by	  others,	  and	  
Whereas,	  the	  Registrar’s	  Office	  currently	  includes	  the	  OER	  designation	  as	  one	  of	  the	  “attributes”	  assigned	  to	  
credit-­‐bearing	  courses,	  and	  
Whereas,	  OER	  programs	  reduce	  the	  out	  of	  pocket	  costs	  to	  students	  while	  ensuring	  high	  quality	  texts	  and	  reading	  
materials,	  and	  
Whereas,	  SUNY	  has	  renewed	  statewide	  funding	  for	  OER	  initiatives	  for	  the	  2018-­‐2019	  academic	  year,	  and	  
Whereas,	  the	  Faculty	  bears	  primary	  responsibility	  for	  instruction	  and	  the	  curriculum,	  of	  which	  OER	  programs	  and	  
materials	  constitute	  a	  meaningful	  component,	  	  
Let	  it	  therefore	  be	  	  
Resolved,	  that	  an	  Ad	  Hoc	  Open	  Educational	  Resources	  (OER)	  Committee	  be	  constituted	  for	  the	  purposes	  of:	  
•  
•  
•  
•  
•  
•  
•  

Creating	  policies	  and	  procedures	  for	  developing	  and	  implementing	  OER	  curricula;	  	  	  	  
Assessing	  course	  syllabi	  to	  determine	  whether	  they	  qualify	  to	  be	  designated	  an	  OER	  course,	  according	  to	  
established	  criteria;	  
Setting	  policies	  for	  the	  allocation	  of	  SUNY	  OER	  funding	  (as	  available)	  to	  support	  campus	  OER	  initiatives;	  	  
Assessing	  needs	  and	  recommending	  resources	  to	  support	  faculty	  teaching	  OER	  courses;	  	  	  	  	  
Cultivating	  resources	  to	  support	  the	  College’s	  OER	  programs;	  	  
Developing	  relationships	  across	  the	  campus	  to	  sustain	  a	  long-­‐term	  OER	  program;	  and	  
Reporting	  to	  the	  Senate	  at	  least	  once	  each	  semester	  on	  OER	  activities,	  promotion,	  and	  sustainability	  
initiatives;	  and,	  let	  it	  further	  be	  

Resolved,	  that	  the	  membership	  of	  the	  Ad	  Hoc	  OER	  Committee	  shall	  include:	  
•  
•  

•  
•  
•  

At	  least	  two	  members	  who	  are	  full-­‐time,	  tenure-­‐stream	  faculty	  who	  have	  taught	  at	  least	  one	  OER	  course,	  
appointed	  by	  the	  Senate	  Chair	  with	  consent	  of	  the	  Senate,	  and	  
At	  least	  two	  members	  who	  are	  full-­‐time,	  tenure-­‐stream	  librarians	  who	  have	  knowledge	  and	  experience	  
with	  OER	  resources,	  including	  the	  OER	  Librarian	  (who	  shall	  chair	  the	  committee),	  if	  such	  has	  been	  
designated,	  appointed	  by	  the	  Senate	  Chair	  with	  consent	  of	  the	  Senate,	  and	  
At	  least	  one	  member	  who	  is	  a	  full	  or	  part-­‐time	  undergraduate	  student,	  to	  be	  designated	  by	  the	  SGA	  
Executive	  Board,	  and	  	  
One	  ex	  officio	  (non-­‐voting)	  member,	  who	  shall	  be	  the	  College’s	  Instructional	  Designer	  (or	  designee),	  and	  
One	  ex	  officio	  (non-­‐voting)	  member,	  to	  be	  designated	  as	  the	  OER	  liaison	  for	  Academic	  Affairs,	  and,	  let	  it	  
further	  be	  

Resolved,	  that	  the	  Ad	  Hoc	  OER	  Committee	  shall	  be	  charged	  to	  recommend	  to	  the	  Faculty	  Senate	  such	  policies	  and	  
procedures	  as	  it	  deems	  suitable	  for	  the	  success	  and	  continued	  development	  of	  OER	  programs,	  and,	  let	  it	  further	  
be	  
Resolved,	  that	  the	  Senate	  shall	  assess	  the	  continued	  role	  for	  an	  Ad	  Hoc	  OER	  Committee	  by	  the	  end	  of	  spring,	  
2019.
